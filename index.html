"I was tired, so I took a break and drank some mate." daily

"I was having a call when the internet died." tech daily

4 · Grammar: Past Simple vs Past Continuous

⏱ 12 min
Past simple = a finished action ✔️   "I fixed the bug."
Past continuous = an action in progress at a moment in the past 🔄   was / were + verb-ing   "I was fixing the bug."

Together — a long action interrupted by a short action:
"I was testing the code when the laptop crashed." 🔄⚡
(was testing = long action in progress · crashed = short action that interrupts it)
Past simplePast continuous
✔️ +I worked / I wentI was working / They were working
❌ −I didn't workI wasn't working
❓ ?Did you work?Were you working?

6 · Listening: Victoria's Terrible Monday

⏱ 10 min
Situation: A programmer called Vicky tells her friend about a terrible Monday. Listen and answer the questions.

Before listening: What can go wrong on a Monday? Guess three things!
🎧 Listen to Vicky's story

📍 Link

Sofi: Hi Vicky! How was your Monday?

Vicky: Terrible! Don't ask... OK, ask.

Sofi: Ha! What happened?

Vicky: Well, at eight o'clock I was drinking my first coffee and reading my emails. Everything was normal. Then, at nine, I logged in to start working... and the system was down.

Sofi: Oh no. What did you do?

Vicky: I turned my laptop off and on again — the classic! But it didn't work. So I called Martín, from my team.

Sofi: And what was he doing?

Vicky: He was having breakfast! But he helped me. While we were looking for the problem, we found a big bug in the new code.

Sofi: A bug? Who made the mistake?

Vicky: Me! I wrote that code on Friday at six p.m. Lesson number one: don't write code on Friday at six p.m.

Sofi: Ha ha! So, did you fix it?

Vicky: Yes, we fixed the bug at midday. Then I took a break, drank more coffee... and the internet died. I'm not joking!

Sofi: Poor Vicky! What a day!

Vicky: Yes. At ten p.m. I was watching a series and eating pizza — the best part of my Monday!

Comprehension questions

After listening (speaking): Did you ever have a day like Vicky's? What happened? Use the past!
